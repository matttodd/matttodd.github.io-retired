I grew up in Long Island, New York with my parents and older sister. Throughout my childhood, I never got to travel much outside the occasional vacation a couple states away. Between junior high and high school, I was finally able to explore more of the United States through academic competitions. Going into college, I knew I wanted to have some experience abroad. I spent half of my summer break after freshman year in Korea, studying and doing research at Yonsei University. The experience abroad in a totally foreign culture was experience I wouldn't trade for anything.

Health has always been an important factor of my life which has lead me to spend a lot of time being active. Most notably of late, I play basketball and racquetball in addition to being part of Northeastern's Obstacle Course Racing team: Elite Heat. This club has introduced me to the whole spectrum of personal wellness and we traveled to all sorts of gyms throughout Boston. We've trained at an actual American Ninja Warrior gym and frequent a bouldering gym just to name a few.

In high school, Science Olympiad and FIRST Robotics were where most of my free time went. During my 6 years with Sci-O, I went to 3 national competitions and took home 2 national medals. My year in the FIRST Tech Challenge brought me and my team to the World Competition. These clubs introduced me to technology as a hobby and passion rather than merely subjects of school, as well as served as competitive fields for me crush other equally nerdy teens.
